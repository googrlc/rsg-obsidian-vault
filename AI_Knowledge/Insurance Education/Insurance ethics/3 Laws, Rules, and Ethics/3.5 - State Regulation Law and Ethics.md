As noted previously, insurance is regulated by state insurance authorities. One of their primary functions is to ensure insurers doing business in that state are properly licensed. Regulators conduct periodic examinations of insurers’ financial records, accounting methods, and processes to make sure the companies are financially healthy. Insurance regulators also oversee insurers’ claim handling practices. Many states have enacted unfair claims practices that require insurers to handle claims promptly and equitably. The laws bar unfair acts like offering unreasonable settlements, changing the policy terms without the insured, and refusing to investigate claims.

Here are some other functions state insurance departments typically perform.

- Establishing and enforcing insurance regulations

- Overseeing market conduct of insurers licensed in the state

- Overseeing agent and broker licensing

- Reviewing and approving insurance rates and form filings

- Influencing lawmaking at state and federal levels

- Regulating advertising and marketing practices
