State and federal laws represent the _minimum_ standards agents should meet when conducting business. However, they may not be adequate standards for making ethical decisions. Illegal acts are unethical, but compliance with the law does not always equate to ethical behavior. In other words, completely legal actions may still be unethical.

Ethics go beyond the minimum standard to promote optimal behavior, i.e., a baseline for ethical behavior. Ethical behavior abides by the spirit and the letter of the law.

This chapter will explore the various laws, written and unwritten, that govern the insurance agent’s actions and business dealings. Because each state has its own laws and regulations governing agent conduct, we will focus on basic regulatory compliance measures that are required of all agents.

This chapter will examine an agent’s responsibility to clients and insurers through various rules and tenets and the impact on ethical behavior.

### Chapter Objectives

Upon completion of this chapter, the student should be able to

- describe the law of agency and how it applies to insurance agents;
- understand state regulatory expectations;
- understand consumer protection laws; and
- understand federal regulations, including privacy laws; and
- understand how laws and regulations can help guide ethical behaviors.
