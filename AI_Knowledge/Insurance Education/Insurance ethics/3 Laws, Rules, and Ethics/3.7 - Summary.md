While they do not define the term ethics, laws and regulations help states and the federal government enforce actions and practices that meet the standards of ethical behavior. Independent agents are expected to behave ethically when interacting with insurers, customers, and others in the insurance industry.

The law of agency is a legal doctrine that controls the relationship between the agent and the insurer. An independent agent typically operates according to a written contract with each insurer they represent. The contract generally affords the agent both express and implied authority to perform various tasks. Agents are expected to adequately represent the insurer and conduct themselves with utmost faith and professionalism when dealing with customers.

State insurance authorities create and enforce laws pertaining to the sale and servicing of insurance and manage all agent, broker, and insurer licensing activities.

Federal laws apply to areas not regulated by the states, including consumer protection, fair trade practices, privacy, and discriminatory actions.

State and federal laws serve as guidelines for the minimum standards of ethical behavior.
