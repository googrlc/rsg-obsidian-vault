Many companies have embraced the idea of managing ethics within the workplace. In an increasingly litigious society, a written code of ethics has become critical to the survival of the business. As businesses bring on more employees, rules of conduct and policies and procedures become more crucial to business continuity. As noted previously in this course, ethical structures can help companies achieve regulatory compliance, reach business goals, guide workplace behaviors, and avoid litigation. A sensibly crafted code of ethics can highlight the company’s primary ethical concerns, guide decision-making, and provide an avenue for reporting and handling potential problems. It can also help a company increase its public persona, which can improve shareholder confidence and consumer loyalty.
