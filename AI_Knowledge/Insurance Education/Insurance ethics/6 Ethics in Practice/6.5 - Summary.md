In a business world with an ever-increasing emphasis on transparency, rules of conduct and policies and procedures become more crucial to business continuity.

Creating an ethical culture starts with creating the framework by which agents and employees will conduct themselves. Policies that help define expected behaviors have measurable value in both internal and external relationships.

When structuring an ethical code, companies should ensure it complies with regulations and laws. Second, the code should be used to define and promote corporate behaviors, both from employees and management. Third, codes of ethics should help companies attain their business goals, perhaps by reducing litigation costs or improving customer retention.

When created from a foundation that takes all these factors into account, a code of ethics can be a go-to guide for management and employees alike. Codes can include decision-making processes, reporting requirements and steps, and the elements required to create an ethically sound corporate culture.
