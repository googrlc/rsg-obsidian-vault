Insurance agents and brokers are regulated by the states. Before they can sell insurance, agents must obtain an insurance license by taking state-approved courses and passing a written exam. Most states require agents to complete continuing education as a condition of maintaining their license. Continuing education courses often address subjects such as agents’ legal duties, agent-company relationships, agent-client relations, and ethics. Agents must also follow state insurance laws. To ensure they remain in compliance, agents must know and understand the laws and keep current on changes.

In addition to the states, insurers may impose ethical standards on agents. Generally, insurers expect agents requirements and conduct themselves in a legal and ethical manner. Many insurers have a compliance department that can provide assistance to agents. Yet, all too often, agents make simple, seemingly minor mistakes that land them on the wrong side of compliance.

For example, an insurance agent, Janet, receives a claim on Monday morning from her client, Robert, for damages his business sustained during a windstorm. Janet waits until Friday to file the claim with the insurer, reasoning that her other clients will be filing similar claims, and she would rather file them all at once to save time. However, the law in Janet’s state requires that agents file all claims within 24 hours of receiving them. Janet is in violation of state law.
