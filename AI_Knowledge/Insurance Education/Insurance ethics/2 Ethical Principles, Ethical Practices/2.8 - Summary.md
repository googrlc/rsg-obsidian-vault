
When agents behave unethically, their actions can have serious legal ramifications for them and the companies they represent. Agents have a responsibility to their clients, their companies, each other, and the general public to conduct themselves with honesty, integrity, and competency.

Agents must comply with the ethical standards of their profession and the rules of conduct established by the companies for which they work. Agents must avoid committing fraudulent acts in any form, including twisting, churning, windowing, and other behaviors intended to deceive clients or insurers. When recommending products for their clients, agents must avoid the temptation of selling higher-commissioned products, steering clients to products by companies that pay better commissions, or changing premium prices to benefit the agent’s needs.

Another area of concern—advertising and marketing—must be handled with meticulous attention to detail. Agents should understand what their state’s regulations are regarding advertising and should know how those laws differ from product to product. All statements contained in advertisements and marketing materials must be honest, must not contain misleading statements, and must not disparage another agent or company.
