Through training, guidance, and practice, agents and their agencies can become more comfortable with, even confident about, making tough ethical decisions. While some paths to ethical compliance and doing the right thing may be straightforward, there are often subtle nuances or complex issues surrounding ethical dilemmas that cloud judgment and make it tough for agents to make the right decision. The best approach to ethical decision-making is to address each problem as a unique circumstance. Because not all issues and scenarios are identical, no one solution or process will fit every problem.

Companies can help their agents and employees become more ethically aware by offering regular training and by building an ethics review process into meetings and conversations. Likewise, companies and agents alike can learn from the decisions others have made. Capturing the knowledge of lessons learned can be a useful tool in addressing the ongoing, ever-changing ethical climate.
