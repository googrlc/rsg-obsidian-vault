In theory, ethical behavior is not hard to achieve. In practice, however, there are many gray areas in which an agent can quickly lose sight of the most appropriate answer. Many ethical quandaries have multiple answers. The goal for the agent is to find the “most right” answer or the solution that will meet the requirements of the company, the regulatory body, and the customer, if possible.

### Chapter Objectives

Upon completion of this chapter, the student should be able to

- develop suggestions for training tools,
- understand fundamentals of an ethical dilemma checklist,
- demonstrate and understanding of questioning guidelines, and
- understand various practice scenarios.
