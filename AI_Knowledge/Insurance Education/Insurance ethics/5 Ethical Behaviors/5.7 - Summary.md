Businesses are beginning to understand that ethical practices can, and must, be managed. In order to maintain sound business operations, companies are looking beyond regulations and implementing their own codes of conduct that dictate ethical expectations. Companies are building top-down ethical cultures that synchronize both business policies and regulatory requirements.

Likewise, agents are expected to possess and demonstrate qualities that augment the insurer’s ethical stance. Insurers are expecting agents to perform their duties with competency and care and to conduct themselves professionally and responsibly when interacting with clients.

Today’s insurance agents will be competent, educated, and fiscally responsible and will keep the client’s needs at the forefront of every sale. Agents will also be expected to perform adequate due diligence to ensure that the products sold to the client will best serve his or her needs. Also, agents are expected to be proactive in their educational pursuits and stay on top of an ever-changing marketplace and regulatory environment.
