Knowing what to do is not the same as doing what one ought to do. Despite laws, policies, and regulations helping to govern ethical behavior in insurance dealings, agents continue to face situations in which the right thing may not feel like the right thing or where ethical lines are blurred. Understanding ethics in theory is much easier than handling ethical situations in the real world.

Agents may make ethical missteps when they are trying to please clients or simply conducting business, unaware that their actions are unethical or in violation of various laws or codes of ethics. This chapter will detail a few of these situations and show how human nature can cause agents to inadvertently cross ethical boundaries.

### Chapter Objectives

Upon completion of this chapter, the student should be able to

- understand ways in which agents can learn to recognize and sidestep problem areas in order to remain ethically compliant,
- understand different ways to make ethical decisions,
- understand the climate within which agents and managers work, and
- be able to explain unintended fraud.
